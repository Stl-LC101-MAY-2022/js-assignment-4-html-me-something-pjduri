Safety Third (But Still on the List)

When I first heard that term, I was at Burning Man. Going by "Safety First" would involve *not* putting burning torches in my mouth or dancing around while spinning fireballs on chains.
But what's the fun in that?
Sometimes, it's fun to do dangerous things. And fire performing is certainly not the only form of creative expression that carries with it a measure of danger. Consider the sorts of injuries a professional ballet dancer can sustain over the course of a career. The row of blisters I got on my lower lip that one time...
...
... those two times I lit my beard on fire are minor compared to broken bones.

That having been said, "dangerous" is not the same as "reckless." I work with safety spotters (and, in fact, started out as a safety spotter myself). Other considerations include wearing natural fibers (polyester + fire = bad for the skin) and dealing with the excess fuel in my wicks before starting a routine.
